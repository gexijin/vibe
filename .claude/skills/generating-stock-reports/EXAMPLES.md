## Example 1: Technology Company (Apple Inc.)

### Command
```
/stock-report AAPL
```

### Generated Report

```markdown

## Executive Summary

Apple Inc. continues to demonstrate strong performance in its core product lines while expanding into new markets. Recent quarterly earnings exceeded analyst expectations, driven by robust iPhone 15 sales and growing services revenue. The company announced significant AI integrations across its product ecosystem and expanded its manufacturing capacity outside of China. Management remains focused on innovation in mixed reality and health technology sectors.

## Product & Service Developments

### iPhone and Hardware
- **iPhone 15 Series**: Strong adoption of iPhone 15 Pro models with titanium design and advanced camera systems
- **Vision Pro**: Mixed reality headset seeing steady enterprise adoption since launch
- **Apple Watch Series 9**: New health monitoring features including sleep apnea detection approved by FDA

### Software and Services
- **Apple Intelligence**: New AI features integrated into iOS 18, including enhanced Siri capabilities
- **Services Growth**: Apple Music, Apple TV+, and iCloud showing double-digit subscriber growth
- **App Store**: New developer tools for spatial computing applications

### Strategic Initiatives
- Partnership with major healthcare providers for Apple Watch health monitoring
- Expansion of Apple Pay into new international markets
- Investment in renewable energy for supply chain operations

## Management & Leadership

### Leadership Team
- **CEO Tim Cook**: Emphasized AI as "huge opportunity" across product line in recent earnings call
- **CFO Luca Maestri**: Announced plans to transition from CFO role in early 2025; successor named
- **COO Jeff Williams**: Leading supply chain diversification initiatives

### Strategic Announcements
- Board approved $110 billion share buyback program
- Increased quarterly dividend by 4%
- Commitment to carbon neutrality across entire supply chain by 2030

### Management Philosophy
- Focus on privacy as competitive differentiator in AI era
- Continued investment in R&D (6.8% of revenue)
- Emphasis on services and recurring revenue streams

## Financial Performance

### Q4 2024 Results (Latest Quarter)
- **Revenue**: $89.5 billion (up 8% year-over-year)
- **Earnings Per Share**: $1.46 (beat estimates of $1.39)
- **iPhone Revenue**: $43.8 billion (up 5%)
- **Services Revenue**: $22.3 billion (up 16%)
- **Gross Margin**: 45.2% (expanded 50 basis points)

### Key Financial Metrics
- **Market Cap**: $3.1 trillion (largest in the world)
- **Cash Position**: $162 billion in cash and marketable securities
- **Free Cash Flow**: $26 billion in Q4
- **P/E Ratio**: 31.2x (forward)

### Guidance
- Expects revenue growth in high single digits for Q1 2025
- Services segment projected to maintain double-digit growth
- Foreign exchange expected to be 2-point headwind

## Analyst Insights

### Ratings and Price Targets
- **Consensus Rating**: Buy (32 Buy, 8 Hold, 2 Sell ratings)
- **Average Price Target**: $215 (16% upside from current levels)
- **Price Target Range**: $175 - $250

### Recent Analyst Actions
- **Morgan Stanley**: Upgraded to Overweight, PT $230 (Dec 10)
- **JPMorgan**: Maintained Overweight, raised PT to $225 (Dec 8)
- **Goldman Sachs**: Maintained Buy, PT $220 (Dec 5)

### Key Analyst Themes
1. **AI Integration**: Analysts bullish on Apple Intelligence driving upgrade cycle
2. **Services Moat**: Recurring revenue provides earnings stability
3. **Valuation**: Premium multiple justified by ecosystem strength
4. **China Risk**: Some concern about competitive pressure from local brands

### Market Sentiment
- Institutional ownership: 61.2% of shares
- Short interest: 0.8% of float (relatively low)
- Options activity: Moderately bullish with call/put ratio of 1.4

## Risk Factors

### Competitive Risks
- Intensifying competition in China from Huawei and Xiaomi
- Android manufacturers catching up in AI features
- Regulatory pressure on App Store economics in EU

### Market Risks
- High valuation leaves little room for disappointment
- Dependence on iPhone for ~50% of revenue
- Slower replacement cycles for smartphones

### Operational Risks
- Supply chain complexity despite diversification efforts
- Currency headwinds from strong dollar
- R&D expenses increasing with AI and Vision Pro development

## Conclusion

Apple maintains its position as a best-in-class technology company with strong fundamentals and multiple growth vectors. The integration of AI across its ecosystem, combined with steady services growth, positions the company well for continued success. While valuation is full, the strength of the ecosystem and management execution support the premium multiple. Investors should monitor China sales trends and AI feature adoption rates.
```

## Usage Notes

### Customization Tips

1. **Adjust Focus**: Ask for emphasis on specific areas
   ```
   /stock-report MSFT focusing on cloud business
   ```

2. **Multiple Companies**: Generate comparative reports
   ```
   Compare NVDA and AMD in the AI chip market
   ```

3. **Specific Timeframe**: Request historical context
   ```
   /stock-report TSLA including last 12 months of developments
   ```

### Best Practices Demonstrated

- **Clear structure**: Consistent sections across all reports
- **Data-driven**: Specific metrics and numbers cited
- **Balanced**: Both positive developments and risk factors
- **Sourced**: All information linked to credible sources
- **Actionable**: Helps users understand key developments

### Quality Markers

✓ Recent information (within 3-6 months)
✓ Multiple credible sources cited
✓ Specific numbers and dates
✓ Balanced perspective
✓ Clear, accessible writing
✓ Proper markdown/HTML formatting
✓ Executive summary provides quick overview
✓ Analyst insights include consensus view
